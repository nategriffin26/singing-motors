## The Strategy

Sell one-off song requests: someone pays $12-$15, tells you what song they want, and you play it on your stepper motor orchestra and post the video to Instagram. You tag them, they share it, their followers discover you, some of them request songs too. Every sale funds a piece of content. Every piece of content drives more sales.

This is not a subscription. No recurring commitment for the buyer, no recurring fulfillment pressure for you. Each transaction is self-contained and produces a shareable asset (the Reel).

The request system is supported by DM automation (someone comments a keyword on your Reel and instantly gets a DM with the link), email capture (building an owned audience for future products), and a simple landing page that ties it all together.

## Why This Works

### The flywheel

```
Fan sees Reel
  -> wants to hear their song
    -> pays $12
      -> you play it and post the video
        -> tag the requester
          -> they share it to their Story
            -> their followers discover you
              -> some of them request songs
                -> cycle repeats
```

Every transaction produces content. Every piece of content produces transactions. This compounds.

### Why per-request beats subscription

| Factor | Per-request | Subscription (Patreon) |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| Buyer friction | Low — impulse buy, no commitment | Higher — monthly commitment |
| Your obligation | Play the song whenever you get to it | Implied ongoing content obligation |
| Content production | Each payment = one Reel | Payments don't map to content |
| Shareability | Requester shares "their" video | No tagging/sharing incentive |
| Scalability | Raise price if demand exceeds supply | Tier restructuring is awkward |

### Why DM automation matters

Bio links get 2-3% click-through rate. DM automation gets 12-18%. That's a 6x improvement. The reason: clicking a bio link requires 6 steps (visit profile, find link, tap, load browser, navigate, act). Commenting a keyword and receiving a DM requires 2 steps.

Additionally, DM links get opened at 10-25% rates vs 2-5% for bio links. You're meeting people where they already are (Instagram DMs) instead of asking them to leave the app.

### Why email capture matters even though the main product is song requests

Instagram is rented land. Algorithm changes can destroy your reach overnight. An email list is yours forever. Even if you only capture emails as a secondary action, you're building an asset for:

- Future product launches (the build guide, firmware bundles, etc.)
- Announcing new song request availability if you implement limited slots
- Re-engaging followers who stopped seeing your posts due to algorithm changes

The conversion math: Instagram follower to buyer is ~1%. Email subscriber to buyer is 2.6-10%. Every email captured is 3-10x more valuable than a follower.

## The Funnel

There are two paths into the funnel. Both run simultaneously.

### Path 1: DM Automation (high conversion)

```
Viewer watches Reel
  -> comments "REQUEST" (or "SONG", "PLAY")
    -> ManyChat auto-DMs them with:
       - Song request link (Ko-fi)
       - Free parts list link (email capture)
    -> ManyChat auto-replies to comment: "Just sent you a DM!"
```

### Path 2: Bio Link (passive, always-on)

```
Viewer visits profile
  -> sees bio with CTA
    -> taps link to Carrd landing page
      -> Option A: Request a song (Ko-fi link)
      -> Option B: Get free parts list (email capture)
      -> Option C: Buy the full build guide (Gumroad link)
```

### After purchase (both paths)

```
Buyer submits song request + payment on Ko-fi
  -> You get email notification with song name
    -> Add to your filming queue
      -> Batch film 3-5 requests per session
        -> Post each as a Reel, tag the requester
          -> Requester shares to their Story (free reach)
```

| Function | Tool | Cost | Why this one |
|----------|------|------|-------------|
| Song request payments | Ko-fi Commissions | Free (5% fee) or $6/mo Gold (0% fee) | Built for commissions: has slots, add-ons, buyer text input, order management. Lower fees than Gumroad (which charges 10% + $0.50). |

**Total monthly cost to start: ~$15/mo** (ManyChat Pro) + $19/year (Carrd) = roughly $17/month.

If you want to validate before spending anything: ManyChat free tier + Ko-fi free tier + Kit free tier = $0/month. You lose the "any post" keyword trigger (free ManyChat only works on specific posts) and pay 5% to Ko-fi, but you can test the entire system for free.

## Setup Guide

### What you'll do vs. what an agent can help with

Most of this setup involves logging into platforms, clicking through UIs, and connecting accounts — it has to be done manually because it requires your credentials and account access. However, there are pieces an agent can prepare for you:

- Writing all the copy (product descriptions, DM messages, bio text, landing page text, email sequences)
- Creating the lead magnet PDF (parts list / BOM)
- Designing the Carrd landing page structure
- Writing the email nurture sequence

The step-by-step below tells you exactly what to click. All the copy you'll need is in the [Copy Templates](#copy-templates) section at the end.

### Phase 1: Instagram Account Setup

**Goal:** Ensure your account is a Professional account (required for DM automation) and connected to a Facebook Page.

**Time: ~5 minutes**

#### Steps

1. Open Instagram on your phone
2. Tap your **profile picture** (bottom-right)
3. Tap the **hamburger menu** (☰, top-right)
4. Tap **Settings and Privacy**
5. Scroll to **Account type and tools**
6. If you see "Switch to professional account":
   - Tap it
   - Choose a category: **"Musician/Band"** or **"Creator"** (you can change this later)
   - Choose **Creator** (not Business) — Creator accounts get full access to Instagram's music library for Reels, which matters for your content. Business accounts are restricted to commercial-use music only.
   - Add contact info (email is enough) — this is optional but recommended
   - When prompted to connect a Facebook Page:
     - If you have one: connect it
     - If you don't: tap **"Create a new Facebook Page"** and follow the prompts. Name it the same as your Instagram handle. This takes 2 minutes and is required for ManyChat to work.
   - Tap **Done**
7. If you already see "Professional account" or "Creator" — you're set. Just verify a Facebook Page is connected:
   - Go to **Settings and Privacy > Account type and tools > Linked accounts** (or **Meta accounts center > Accounts > Add accounts**)
   - Confirm a Facebook Page is linked

**Checkpoint:** You should now see **"Professional dashboard"** or **"Insights"** on your profile. If you see Insights, you're ready for Phase 5 (ManyChat).

### Phase 2: Ko-fi Song Request Product

**Goal:** Create a commission listing where people pay and submit their song request.

**Time: ~15 minutes**

#### Steps

1. Go to [ko-fi.com](https://ko-fi.com) and sign up (or log in)
2. During onboarding (or in Settings), connect a payment method:
   - Go to **Settings > Payment Settings**
   - Connect **Stripe** (recommended — fastest payouts, widest card support) and/or **PayPal**
   - Payments go directly to you, instantly. No waiting for Ko-fi to release funds.
3. In the left sidebar, click **Commissions**
4. If this is your first time, you may need to enable commissions. Look for an "Enable" or "Get Started" button.
5. Click **"Add Listing"** (or "Create New Commission")
6. Fill in the listing:

   **Title:**
   > Song Request — Stepper Motor Music

   **Description:** (see [Copy Templates](#copy-templates) for full text)
   > Pick any song. I'll play it on my 8-motor stepper orchestra and post the video to Instagram. I'll tag you when it goes live.

   **Price:** $12 (or $15 if you want a higher anchor — you can always lower it later)

   **Instructions to Buyer** (this is the field where they type their song name):
   > What song would you like me to play? Please include the song title and artist name. If there's a specific version or part of the song you want, mention that too.

   **Slots:** Start with 10. This creates scarcity ("7 of 10 slots remaining") and prevents you from getting buried in requests. You can always increase this. Setting it to 0 marks the listing as unavailable — useful if you need to pause.

   **Preview images:** Upload 1-2 images:
   - A still frame from one of your best Reels (motors spinning, dashboard visible)
   - Optionally a simple graphic showing "Your Song + Stepper Motors = Video"

   **Terms of Service** (optional but recommended):
   > Some songs work better than others on stepper motors (melodies and bass lines sound best; heavily percussive or vocal-only tracks may not translate well). If your song doesn't work well, I'll reach out with alternatives or offer a full refund. Typical turnaround is 1-2 weeks. The finished video will be posted to my Instagram — by purchasing, you're giving me permission to post it publicly.

7. Enable **"Pay What You Want"** if you want people to be able to tip above your base price (recommended — some people will pay $20-$25 for a $12 listing if they're excited)
8. Click **Save** / **Publish**
9. Grab your listing URL. It will look like: `https://ko-fi.com/yourusername/commissions`

#### Optional: Add-ons

Ko-fi supports add-ons on commission listings. Consider:
- **Priority processing (+$10):** "Your song moves to the front of the queue — filmed within the next 3 videos"
- This effectively creates the two-tier system ($12 standard / $22 priority) without needing a separate product

#### Optional: Rename "Commissions"

If "Commissions" feels off-brand:
1. Go to **Commissions > Additional Settings**
2. Under **"Change 'Commissions' Wording"**, select **"Requests"**
3. Your page now says "Requests" instead of "Commissions" — feels more natural for song requests

**Checkpoint:** Visit your Ko-fi page. You should see your song request listing with the price, description, and a button to order. Test the checkout flow yourself (you can cancel before paying) to make sure the "Instructions to Buyer" text field appears.

### Phase 3: Email Capture with Kit

**Goal:** Set up email collection so you can build a list alongside song request sales.

**Time: ~20 minutes**

#### Steps

1. Go to [kit.com](https://kit.com) and sign up for the **free plan**
2. During onboarding, Kit will ask about your audience size and goals. Choose whatever fits — it just customizes the dashboard slightly.
3. **Create a Form** (this is what Carrd will connect to):
   - Go to **Grow > Landing Pages & Forms** in the left nav
   - Click **+ Create new**
   - Select **Form** (not Landing Page — Carrd will be your landing page)
   - Choose **Inline** display format
   - Pick any template (it doesn't matter much since this form will be embedded in Carrd, not used standalone)
   - Customize the fields:
     - Keep **Email** (required)
     - Optionally add **First Name**
   - Under **Settings > Incentive**:
     - Toggle on **"Send incentive email"**
     - Upload your lead magnet PDF (the free parts list / BOM) — Kit will auto-send it to new subscribers
     - Customize the incentive email subject line: "Here's your stepper motor parts list"
   - Click **Save**
   - Note the **Form ID** — you'll find it in the URL when editing the form (e.g., `app.kit.com/forms/XXXXXX`). You'll need this for the Carrd integration.

4. **Get your API Key** (needed for Carrd integration):
   - Go to **Settings** (gear icon)
   - Click **Developer**
   - Under **API Keys**, find or generate your **V4 API Key**
   - Copy it somewhere safe (you'll paste it into Carrd in Phase 4)

5. **Create a simple welcome email sequence** (optional but recommended):
   - Go to **Send > Sequences**
   - Click **+ Create Sequence**
   - Name it "Song Request Welcome"
   - Email 1 (sends immediately after signup):
     - Subject: "Your stepper motor parts list is here"
     - Body: Deliver the PDF link again (some people miss the incentive email), introduce yourself briefly, link to your Instagram
   - Email 2 (sends 3 days later):
     - Subject: "How stepper motor music actually works"
     - Body: Brief explanation of the ESP32 + MIDI pipeline. Link to your best Reel. Mention the build guide exists.
   - Email 3 (sends 7 days later):
     - Subject: "Want to hear your favorite song on stepper motors?"
     - Body: Explain the song request system. Link directly to Ko-fi listing. This is your soft sell.
   - For each email: toggle on at the top of the editor, set the delay, write the content
   - Under **Settings** for the sequence: set the "From" name and email

6. **Connect the form to the sequence**:
   - Go back to your form (Grow > Landing Pages & Forms > click your form)
   - Under **Settings > Automations** (or via the main Automations page):
     - Create a rule: "When someone subscribes via [this form], add them to [Song Request Welcome sequence]"
   - This ensures every new email subscriber gets the 3-email nurture automatically

**Checkpoint:** Send yourself a test submission through the form. You should receive the incentive email with the parts list PDF, and then (if you set delays to "0 days" for testing) the follow-up sequence emails.

### Phase 4: Landing Page with Carrd

**Goal:** Create a simple, fast-loading page that serves as your bio link destination. It should offer the song request, the free parts list (email capture), and the build guide.

**Time: ~30 minutes**

#### Steps

1. Go to [carrd.co](https://carrd.co) and sign up
2. Start the **7-day free trial of Pro Standard** ($19/year after trial) — you need this tier for signup forms
3. Click **"New Site"** from your dashboard
4. **Choose a template:**
   - Filter by **"Profile"** or **"Landing Page"**
   - Pick something clean and dark (matches the aesthetic of your stepper motor content)
   - Keep it simple — you want a single scroll with 3-4 sections max
   - **Important:** You cannot change the template after selecting it, so preview a few before committing

5. **Build the page structure** (click elements to edit, use the "+" button to add new ones):

   **Section 1 — Hero:**
   - Your name / project name as heading
   - One line: "8 stepper motors. 1 ESP32. Any song."
   - A hero image or embedded Reel (your best-performing video)

   **Section 2 — Song Request (primary CTA):**
   - Heading: "Request a Song"
   - Text: "Pick a song, I'll play it on the stepper motors and post the video. I'll tag you when it goes live."
   - Button: **"Request a Song — $12"** → links to `https://ko-fi.com/localhostaudio/commissions`
   - Make this the most visually prominent section (largest button, contrasting color)

   **Section 3 — Free Parts List (email capture):**
   - Heading: "Build Your Own"
   - Text: "Get the free parts list — everything you need to build a stepper motor music player."
   - **Add a Form element:**
     - Click **"+" > Form**
     - Set Type to **"Signup"**
     - Set Provider to **"Kit"**
     - Paste your **Kit V4 API Key**
     - Enter your **Form ID** (from Phase 3)
     - Under Fields: show Email (required) and optionally First Name
     - Button text: **"Send Me the Parts List"**
     - After submission: show a message like "Check your email — the parts list is on its way."

   **Section 4 — Build Guide (secondary CTA):**
   - Heading: "The Full Build Guide"
   - Text: "47 pages. Complete wiring diagrams, firmware walkthrough, and source code. Everything to build the full 8-motor setup."
   - Button: **"Get the Guide"** → links to `https://localhostaudio.gumroad.com/l/code-and-complete-build-guide?wanted=true`

   **Footer:**
   - Links to your Instagram
   - Optional: "Built by [your name]"

6. **Styling:**
   - Dark background (matches your brand/terminal aesthetic)
   - Use your accent color from the dashboard for buttons and highlights
   - Keep fonts clean and readable (system fonts or Google Fonts like Inter, Space Mono, JetBrains Mono)
   - Ensure all buttons are large enough for mobile taps (remember: 85%+ of traffic will be mobile)

7. **Test on mobile:**
   - Click the phone icon in the Carrd editor to preview mobile layout
   - Make sure nothing is cut off, buttons are tappable, and the form works
   - The page should load in under 2 seconds (Carrd is fast by default — don't add heavy images)

8. **Publish:**
   - Click **Publish** in the top-right
   - For now, use the free `yourname.carrd.co` subdomain
   - If you have a custom domain, connect it under **Settings > Domain** (requires Pro Standard)
   - Copy the published URL — you'll use this in your Instagram bio

**Checkpoint:** Visit the published URL on your phone. Test the email form (submit a real email, verify the Kit incentive email arrives). Test the Ko-fi and Gumroad buttons (verify they open correctly in mobile browser).

### Phase 5: DM Automation with ManyChat

**Goal:** When someone comments a keyword (like "REQUEST" or "SONG") on your Reels, they automatically receive a DM with the song request link and the free parts list link.

**Time: ~30 minutes**

#### Prerequisites
- Instagram must be a Professional (Creator or Business) account (Phase 1)
- Instagram must be connected to a Facebook Page (Phase 1)
- Your Ko-fi listing must be live (Phase 2)
- Your Carrd page or Kit form must be live (Phase 3-4)

#### Steps

1. Go to [manychat.com](https://manychat.com) and sign up
2. **Connect your Instagram account:**
   - Go to **Settings > Channels > Instagram**
   - Click **Connect**
   - Log in with the Facebook account that is admin of your linked Facebook Page
   - Grant all requested permissions (ManyChat needs access to your Instagram media and messaging)
   - ManyChat will verify the connection — this may take a minute

3. **Create the Song Request automation:**
   - Go to the **Automation** tab in the left sidebar
   - Click **+ New Automation**
   - Choose **"Start from Scratch"** (or use the template "Convert Instagram Comments to Sales" if available)
   - Click **+ New Trigger**
   - Select **"User comments on your Post or Reel"** (under the Instagram section)

4. **Configure the trigger:**
   - **Free tier:** You'll need to select a **specific post or Reel**. Pick your most recent or best-performing one. (You'll need to create a new automation for each new Reel — annoying but workable for validation. Pro tier at $15/mo lets you set "Any post or Reel.")
   - **Pro tier:** Select **"Any post or Reel"**
   - Under **Keyword settings:**
     - Add keyword: `REQUEST`
     - Add keyword: `SONG`
     - Add keyword: `PLAY`
     - Set matching to **"contains"** (so "can you play this?" triggers on "play")
     - Optional: add the keyword `BUILD` with a different automation (see step 8)

5. **Build the DM message:**
   - After the trigger, add an action: **"Send Message"**
   - Select **"Instagram Direct Message"**
   - Write the message (see [Copy Templates](#copy-templates) for the exact text). Structure:
     - Line 1: Acknowledge their interest with energy
     - Line 2-3: Explain what they get (you play their song, post the video, tag them)
     - Line 4: Link to Ko-fi song request
     - Line 5: Mention the free parts list as a secondary offer
     - Line 6: Link to Carrd landing page (or direct Kit form link)
   - **Add 3-5 message variations** — ManyChat rotates these so the DMs don't look robotic. Each variation should say the same thing in slightly different words.
   - **Include the links as full URLs** (with `https://`) — they'll be tappable in DMs

6. **Set up the auto-comment reply:**
   - In the same automation, look for **"Auto-response"** or add a **"Reply to Comment"** action
   - Write 3-5 variations of a public comment reply:
     - "Just sent you a DM with the link! 🎵"
     - "Check your DMs — just sent you the details!"
     - "DM incoming! Check your message requests 👀"
   - This serves two purposes: it signals to the commenter to check their DMs, and it shows other viewers that commenting gets a response (social proof that encourages more comments)

7. **Connect Kit for email capture** (optional but recommended):
   - Go to **Settings > Integrations**
   - Find **Kit (ConvertKit)** and connect it using your Kit API key
   - Back in your automation flow, you can add a step after the DM:
     - **Action > Kit > Add to Form** — automatically adds the person's email to your Kit list if they provide it through the DM flow
   - Note: This is more advanced and requires the user to share their email in the DM conversation. For simplicity, just linking to the Carrd page (which has the Kit form) works fine.

8. **Create a second automation for the build guide** (optional):
   - Repeat steps 3-6 but with keyword `BUILD` or `GUIDE`
   - The DM message links to the free parts list / Carrd landing page instead of Ko-fi
   - This captures the "I want to build this" audience separately from the "play my song" audience

9. **Activate the automation:**
   - Click **"Set Live"** (or toggle the automation on)
   - The automation will NOT work until this step is done
   - **Test it:** Use a secondary Instagram account (or ask a friend) to comment your keyword on the target post. Verify the DM arrives within a few seconds and the links work.

#### ManyChat Free vs Pro Decision

Start with the free tier to validate. Upgrade to Pro ($15/mo) when:
- You're posting Reels frequently and don't want to create a new automation per post
- You exceed 1,000 contacts
- You want to use more than 3 keyword triggers

**Checkpoint:** Comment "REQUEST" on your target Reel from a different account. Within 10-30 seconds, you should receive a DM with the Ko-fi link and parts list link, and see a public reply on your comment.

#### Pin a "How to Request" Reel

Create a short Reel (15-30 seconds) explaining the song request system. Script:

> "You pick a song. I play it on 8 stepper motors. And I post the video and tag you. Comment REQUEST on any of my videos or tap the link in my bio."

**Every Reel caption should end with one of these CTAs** (rotate them to avoid repetition):

- "Want me to play your song? Comment REQUEST"
- "This was a request from @username — comment SONG to get yours"
- "Comment PLAY and I'll send you the link to request a song"
- "8 motors, any song. Details in my bio."

**In the video itself** (text overlay or spoken, within the first 3 seconds):
- "Someone requested [song name] — here's how it sounds"
- "Song request from @username"

The "someone requested this" framing is powerful because it:
1. Shows social proof (other people are paying for this)
2. Creates desire (I want my song played too)
3. Gives the viewer a reason to watch (reveal/reaction format)
4. Tags the requester naturally (they share it)

## Fulfillment Workflow

This is how you actually handle requests once they start coming in.

### When a request comes in

1. **Ko-fi sends you an email notification** with the buyer's name, payment amount, and their response to your "Instructions to Buyer" field (the song name and artist)
2. **Check if a MIDI file exists:**
   - Search for the MIDI on the sites you normally use
   - If a good MIDI exists: add to your filming queue
   - If no MIDI exists or the song won't translate well (too percussive, too vocal-heavy, etc.): message the buyer on Ko-fi (pre-purchase DMs are built in) and offer alternatives or a refund
3. **Add to your queue** — keep a simple list (Notes app, Notion, whatever). Track: song name, requester Instagram handle, date paid, status (queued / filmed / posted)

### Batch filming

Don't film one request at a time. Accumulate 3-5 requests, then do a filming session:

1. Load the first MIDI file into your setup
2. Start playback
3. Record the video (phone on tripod, capture both the motors and dashboard, or whatever your standard setup is)
4. Repeat for each request
5. You now have 3-5 Reels ready to post over the next 1-2 weeks

### Posting

- Post 1 Reel per day or every other day (don't dump them all at once — spacing maximizes algorithmic reach)
- In the caption, credit the requester: "Song request from @username"
- Tag them in the video and/or caption
- After posting, DM the requester: "Your song is live! Here's the link: [Reel URL]. Thanks for the request 🎵"
- On Ko-fi, mark the commission as complete

### Turnaround time

Set expectations on the Ko-fi listing: "Most requests are filmed within 1-2 weeks." This gives you a comfortable buffer to batch film. If you're getting more requests than you can handle, that's a good problem — see [Scaling and Adjustments](#scaling-and-adjustments).

## Scaling and Adjustments

### If demand is low (0-2 requests/month)

This is normal at the start. Focus on:
- Growing your Instagram through consistent Reels (3-5 per week)
- Making sure the DM automation is active on every new Reel (manually on free ManyChat, or upgrade to Pro)
- Featuring song request videos prominently ("This was a request — want yours? Comment REQUEST")
- The song request system is also a content strategy, not just a revenue strategy. Even 1 request/month gives you a Reel concept you didn't have to think of.

### If demand is moderate (3-8 requests/month)

You're in a good spot. This is $36-$120/month and 3-8 pieces of content. Keep going. Consider:
- Upgrading to ManyChat Pro ($15/mo) if you haven't already
- Upgrading to Ko-fi Gold ($6/mo) to drop the platform fee from 5% to 0%
- Tracking which songs get the most views as requests — this tells you what your audience wants

### If demand is high (10+ requests/month)

Good problem. Options:

1. **Raise the price.** $12 → $15 → $20. The market is telling you the price is too low. This also naturally reduces volume to a manageable level.
2. **Reduce slots.** Set Ko-fi slots to 5-8 per batch. When they sell out, the listing shows "Sold Out" which creates scarcity. Restock every 2 weeks.
3. **Add the priority tier.** Enable the "+$10 priority processing" add-on. People who want it faster pay more. You film priority requests first.
4. **Batch more aggressively.** Film 8-10 at once. With your setup this should be fast — the bottleneck is finding MIDIs and loading them, not the recording itself.

So here's the short version of how the stepper motor music setup works:

1. A MIDI file defines which notes to play and when
2. A Python host on my laptop reads the MIDI and converts it into frequency/timing segments
3. Those segments stream over USB/UART to an ESP32 microcontroller at 921,600 baud
4. The ESP32's pulse engine drives 8 stepper motors at precise frequencies
